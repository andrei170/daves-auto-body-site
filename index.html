Our Story

Dave Wierer opened the doors in 1983. His daughter Jessica runs it today.

Dave Wierer founded Dave's Auto Body Inc at 1748 West Paulson Road in Green Bay in 1983. He worked that bay for 35 years. In 2018, his daughter Jessica Senn took over as owner and president, and the shop kept its name, its address, and the same crew on the floor.

Forty-two years later, the shop is still on Paulson Road. The phone numbers still route to the same building. Most of the technicians on the floor have been there longer than most national chains have had a Green Bay location. A customer who had a fender repaired here in 2008 can bring a teenager's first car back in 2026 and recognize the names on the work order.

"The same address since 1983. The same family. A daughter-led second generation, and a crew with roughly 89 combined years on the bay."

The shop runs collision repair, refinishing, towing, and used-vehicle sales out of one Paulson Road building. The tagline on the wall: "honesty is the best way to gain the trust of customers."

Insurance and Direct Pay

Working with insurance or paying directly: here is what to know.

Dave's Auto Body works with drivers coming in on insurance claims and with drivers paying out of pocket. The process differs, and the shop is straightforward about both paths from the first call.

If you have an insurance claim, bring the claim number and your adjuster's contact information. The shop works through the repair process with the carrier. If you are paying directly, the estimate is written at the shop and the cost is explained before any work begins.

  • Insurance claims: Bring your claim number. The shop coordinates with the adjuster on your behalf.
  • Direct pay: A written estimate before work starts. No surprise additions without your approval.
  • Towing: If the vehicle is not drivable, call (920) 396-0772 first. The tow line routes directly to dispatch.
  • Timeline: The shop gives a repair timeline at the estimate stage. It tells you if that changes.
  • The standard: "Honesty is the best way to gain the trust of customers." That applies to the invoice as much as the repair.
What to Bring

Before you come in, have these ready.

Insurance claim: Your claim number, adjuster name and phone, insurance card, and vehicle registration. The shop takes it from there.


Out-of-pocket repair: The vehicle and a description of what happened. The shop writes the estimate on site. No paperwork required in advance.


Towing first: If the vehicle is not drivable, do not bring it, call it in. The tow line at (920) 396-0772 dispatches from the same Paulson Road location.
